Accelerating DeveLopment Cycles in the era of AI

May 4th, 2026

In a recent episode of Lenny's podcast, Cat Wu shared how Anthropic radically accelerated their product shipping cycles - compressing timeline that once took six months into a week, or even a day. Having recently discussed this topic in one of my interviews, I wanted to share some of my thoughts.

Performing Timeboxed Proof of Concept (PoC)

The primary goal of a prototype is to de-risk an idea before committing significant resources. By implementing strict timeboxing, teams can quickly validate technical feasibility or product-market fit. This "fail fast" mentality ensures that engineering effort is only invested in concepts that have been proven to work in a sandbox environment.

Leverage "Dogfooding" and Beta Interations

Highlighted by Cat Wu, internal testing (dogfooding) and early beta releases are essential for high-speed delivery. Rather than waiting for a "perfect" version, shipping a functional beta to internal teams or a select group of users creates continous feedback loop. This allows for real-time adjustments and ensures the final product aligns with actual user needs. In my previous work, we also used this approach.

Transition to AI-Augmented Code Reviews

With AI now enabling engineers to generate code at unprecedented speeds, the traditional human-led Pull Request process has become a primary bottleneck. To maintain momentum, teams should integrate AI-driven code reviews.

While AI can handle syntax, style, and basic logic checks to significantly increase merge rates, it is crucial to establish clear guardrails. Human oversight should remain focused on high-risk architectural changes and mission-critical systems to ensure production stability isn't sacrificed for speed.
